# Case: Amazon Flywheel

## Snapshot
Amazon's flywheel ties lower prices, wider selection, and faster delivery into a reinforcing loop that accelerates customer growth and merchant participation.

## Mental Models
[[Feedback Loops]] · [[Network Effects]] · [[Compounding]]

## Flywheel Components
1. Better selection attracts more customers.
2. More customers attract more sellers, improving selection.
3. Higher volume drives lower unit costs, enabling lower prices and infrastructure investments.

## Decision Lessons
- Reinforcing loops require upfront investment but compound quickly once spinning.
- Pair flywheels with [[Margin of Safety]] by building cash reserves to endure thin margins.
