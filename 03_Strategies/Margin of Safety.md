# Strategy: Margin of Safety

## Definition
Margin of safety is the buffer between an asset's intrinsic value and the price you pay, protecting against estimation errors and adverse events.

## Core Principle
Even well-researched forecasts are uncertain. Building slack into decisions improves resilience.

## When To Use
- Investing, procurement, or capacity planning.
- Any system where downside risk is asymmetric.

## Models Involved
[[Probability]] · [[Opportunity Cost]] · [[Loss Aversion]]

## Examples
- Buying shares only when priced at least 30% below intrinsic value.
- Adding production redundancy to avoid single points of failure.
