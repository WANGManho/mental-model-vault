# Mental Model: Antifragility

## Definition
Antifragility describes systems that gain from disorder, volatility, or stressors, improving rather than merely surviving shocks.

## Core Principle
By exposing themselves to variability in controlled doses, antifragile systems build optionality and adaptation mechanisms that thrive under stress.

## When To Use
- Portfolio construction mixing small risky bets with safe core holdings.
- Organizational design encouraging experiments and rapid feedback.

## Examples
- Fitness regimens using varied stress to build strength and resilience.
- Product teams running game days to harden infrastructure.

## Related Models
[[Buffering and Slack]] · [[Option Value]] · [[Red Queen Effect]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Highly optimized, brittle systems that fail catastrophically when stressed.
- Cultures that punish experimentation, reducing adaptive capacity.
