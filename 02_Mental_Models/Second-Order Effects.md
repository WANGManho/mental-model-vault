# Mental Model: Second-Order Effects

## Definition
Second-order effects are the downstream consequences that follow the immediate result of an action, often producing unintended outcomes.

## Core Principle
Every intervention changes incentives and system structure; thoughtful decisions anticipate ripple effects beyond the first step.

## When To Use
- Policy design, pricing changes, and org restructuring.
- Evaluating automation or AI deployment on workforce morale.

## Examples
- Price caps leading to shortages because suppliers exit the market.
- Faster release cycles increasing burnout if support capacity is ignored.

## Related Models
[[Systems Thinking]] · [[Tragedy of the Commons]] · [[Cascading Failure]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Simple, isolated tasks with limited interactions.
- Experiments deliberately sandboxed to prevent spillover.

## Sources
- Systems dynamics literature and field experience.
