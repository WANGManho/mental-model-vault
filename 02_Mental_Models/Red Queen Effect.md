# Mental Model: Red Queen Effect

## Definition
The Red Queen effect describes how species or competitors must continuously evolve just to maintain their relative position.

## Core Principle
When everyone adapts simultaneously, standing still equates to falling behind, so continuous improvement is mandatory.

## When To Use
- Competitive markets where rivals rapidly copy features.
- Personal skill development in fast-changing industries.

## Examples
- Cybersecurity teams racing attackers who constantly invent new exploits.
- Streaming platforms endlessly producing new content to retain subscribers.

## Related Models
[[Natural Selection]] · [[Long-term Thinking]] · [[Critical Mass]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Monopolies or regulated utilities with little competitive pressure.
- Unique intellectual property that creates durable moats.

## Sources
- Evolutionary biology and coevolution studies.
