# Mental Model: Incentives

## Definition
Incentives are rewards or penalties that drive behavior; systems get the behavior they pay for.

## Core Principle
People respond to perceived payoff structures, so design incentives carefully or risk gaming and unintended consequences.

## When To Use
- Structuring compensation, pricing, or partnerships.
- Diagnosing why teams or customers act in surprising ways.

## Examples
- Sales quotas emphasizing volume leading to low-margin deals.
- Loyalty programs reinforcing [[Network Effects]].

## Related Models
[[Opportunity Cost]] · [[Loss Aversion]] · [[Comparative Advantage]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Values-driven actions where incentives matter less than identity.
- Complex systems where the signal from incentives is too weak.

## Sources
- Charlie Munger: "Show me the incentive and I will show you the outcome."
