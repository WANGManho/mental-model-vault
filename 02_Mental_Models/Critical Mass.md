# Mental Model: Critical Mass

## Definition
Critical mass is the threshold at which a system gains enough momentum that self-sustaining processes take over.

## Core Principle
Below the threshold, inputs dissipate; once surpassed, reinforcing loops and [[Network Effects]] keep the system growing with less effort.

## When To Use
- Launching marketplaces, communities, or products reliant on user adoption.
- Planning cultural change initiatives looking for tipping points.

## Examples
- Social networks becoming indispensable after reaching local density.
- Renewable energy adoption accelerating once costs fall below grid parity.

## Related Models
[[Feedback Loops]] · [[Compounding]] · [[Leverage Points]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Fragmented audiences preventing density.
- Overly niche products that never cross awareness thresholds.

## Sources
- Nuclear physics and diffusion of innovation theory.
