# Mental Model: Principal-Agent Problem

## Definition
The principal-agent problem arises when people make decisions on behalf of others while their incentives diverge from the owner’s goals.

## Core Principle
Information asymmetry and misaligned rewards cause agents to optimize for themselves, so principals must design contracts, monitoring, or trust-based mechanisms to align outcomes.

## When To Use
- Structuring executive compensation or sales plans.
- Evaluating outsourced work, investment funds, or franchise systems.

## Examples
- Fund managers chasing fees at the expense of long-term returns.
- Sales teams pushing volume regardless of quality because quotas reward revenue only.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Small teams where principals and agents are the same people and information asymmetry is low.
- Cultures with strong shared mission that overrides financial incentives.
