# Mental Model: Natural Selection

## Definition
Natural selection is the process where heritable traits that improve survival or reproduction become more common in a population over generations.

## Core Principle
Variation, selection, and retention drive adaptation, so strategies that iterate on fit tend to dominate over time.

## When To Use
- Product-market fit experiments testing variations.
- Organizational design that rewards effective behaviors and retires ineffective ones.

## Examples
- Startups iterating MVPs, keeping features customers actually use.
- Hiring processes selecting for culture contributors and letting misfits exit.

## Related Models
[[Evolution]] · [[Red Queen Effect]] · [[Feedback Loops]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Environments lacking variation or selection pressure.
- Monopolies protected from competition where maladaptation persists.

## Sources
