# Mental Model: Social Proof

## Definition
Social proof is the tendency to look to others’ behavior or beliefs when deciding what to do, especially under uncertainty.

## Core Principle
Humans assume the crowd knows something they do not, so visible adoption acts as a shortcut for credibility and safety.

## When To Use
- Product launches relying on testimonials, reviews, or user counts.
- Change management initiatives needing cultural buy-in.

## Examples
- Displaying "bestseller" badges to nudge purchases.
- Teams adopting a tool because adjacent departments already use it.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Highly independent experts relying on first-principles evidence.
- Contexts where crowds are manipulated or obviously uninformed.
