# Mental Model: Resonance

## Definition
Resonance occurs when a system vibrates at greater amplitude because external forces match its natural frequency.

## Core Principle
Matching timing and frequency with existing rhythms amplifies impact; mismatches waste energy or cause destructive interference.

## When To Use
- Designing communication cadences that align with stakeholder cycles.
- Launching products or campaigns when market attention peaks.

## Examples
- Shipping features just before industry conferences to ride attention waves.
- Aligning sprint demos with executive meetings to boost visibility.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Random, unsynchronized pushes that fail to move the system.
- Over-resonance leading to runaway oscillations or burnout.
