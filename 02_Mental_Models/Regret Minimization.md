# Mental Model: Regret Minimization

## Definition
Regret minimization frames decisions around which choice you would regret least in the future, emphasizing long-term perspective over short-term comfort.

## Core Principle
Imagining yourself at a later point clarifies values and highlights irreversible trade-offs.

## When To Use
- Career moves, strategic bets, or personal commitments.
- Situations where metrics are fuzzy but values are clear.

## Examples
- Choosing to start a venture now to avoid wondering "what if" later.
- Prioritizing foundational platform work to avoid future technical debt regret.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Highly quantitative decisions where regret is less relevant than expected value.
- Short-lived, reversible choices.
