# Mental Model: Scarcity Effect

## Definition
The scarcity effect makes people assign higher value to opportunities or resources perceived as scarce or time-limited.

## Core Principle
Fear of missing out and loss aversion heighten desire for constrained items, regardless of intrinsic value.

## When To Use
- Marketing campaigns using limited runs or deadlines.
- Prioritizing resource allocations when teams feel stretched.

## Examples
- Countdown timers on e-commerce product launches.
- Exclusive beta invites increasing engagement and feedback.

## Related Models
[[Loss Aversion]] · [[Anchoring]] · [[Tragedy of the Commons]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Commodities with abundant supply where scarcity claims lack credibility.
- Transparent systems that show ample capacity or alternatives.

## Sources
- Behavioral economics research on scarcity and urgency.
