# Mental Model: Cognitive Dissonance

## Definition
Cognitive dissonance is the discomfort felt when actions conflict with beliefs, motivating people to rationalize or change attitudes.

## Core Principle
Humans strive for internal consistency; when presented with contradictions, we adjust stories, preferences, or memories to reduce tension.

## When To Use
- Change initiatives where people must abandon prior commitments.
- Customer journeys requiring adoption of unfamiliar habits.

## Examples
- Investors refusing to sell losers because it conflicts with self-image as competent.
- Teams defending legacy systems despite evidence of better alternatives.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Environments embracing experimentation and admitting mistakes openly.
- Individuals practiced in metacognition and reframing.
