# Mental Model: Opportunity Cost

## Definition
Opportunity cost measures the value of the best alternative forgone when choosing one option over another.

## Core Principle
Resources are scarce. Every allocation implicitly rejects the next-best option, so rational choices compare expected value, not absolute outcomes.

## When To Use
- Prioritizing projects, careers, or investments.
- Evaluating time commitments and meetings.
- Comparing strategic initiatives with limited capital.

## Examples
- Allocating R&D budget to a flagship product means delaying adjacent bets.
- Holding cash during inflation erodes potential returns relative to [[Value Investing]].

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- False dichotomies that ignore creative alternatives.
- Status quo bias that hides the cost of inaction.
