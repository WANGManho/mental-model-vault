# Mental Model: Commitment and Consistency

## Definition
Commitment and consistency describe how people strive to align behaviors with previous statements, promises, or identities.

## Core Principle
Once we publicly commit, we feel pressure to behave consistently to avoid cognitive dissonance, so even small commitments can escalate future compliance.

## When To Use
- Habit formation, onboarding, and goal-setting rituals.
- Product adoption flows that ask users to declare objectives upfront.

## Examples
- Signing a pledge to write daily increases follow-through.
- Customers continuing subscriptions because they self-identify as members.

## Related Models
[[Cognitive Dissonance]] · [[Social Proof]] · [[Regret Minimization]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Anonymous or private choices with little reputational cost.
- Cultures that celebrate flexibility and updating beliefs.
