# Mental Model: Modularity

## Definition
Modularity is the design principle of building systems from interchangeable components with well-defined interfaces.

## Core Principle
Decoupling allows independent development, reduces cascade risk, and lets teams upgrade parts without rewriting the whole.

## When To Use
- Software architecture, hardware design, and organizational structuring.
- Product portfolios where components need to evolve asynchronously.

## Examples
- Microservices enabling separate teams to ship independently.
- Organizational pods owning specific capabilities with clear APIs.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Monolithic systems where any change requires touching the entire structure.
- Ultra-high-performance domains where modular boundaries add unacceptable overhead.
