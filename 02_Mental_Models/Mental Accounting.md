# Mental Model: Mental Accounting

## Definition
Mental accounting describes how people treat money or resources differently depending on arbitrary categories or buckets.

## Core Principle
Instead of optimizing total utility, we create separate mental ledgers (bonus versus salary, capex versus opex) that distort decisions.

## When To Use
- Budget planning and portfolio allocation.
- Evaluating how teams justify expenses or sunk investments.

## Examples
- Celebrating a tax refund as "free money" and spending it frivolously.
- Product teams protecting unused budget even when better opportunities exist elsewhere.

## Related Models
[[Anchoring]] · [[Loss Aversion]] · [[Opportunity Cost]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Integrated financial dashboards that force all resources into a single balance sheet.
- Strict governance requiring trade-off decisions across categories.
