# Mental Model: Expected Value

## Definition
Expected value is the probability-weighted average outcome of a decision across all possible scenarios.

## Core Principle
Rational choices maximize expected value rather than focusing solely on best or worst cases; probabilities and payoffs must be considered jointly.

## When To Use
- Investment decisions, pricing bets, or prioritizing experiments.
- Evaluating trade-offs under uncertainty in product or policy design.

## Examples
- Comparing marketing channels by multiplying conversion uplift by likelihood of success.
- Assessing risk-adjusted returns in [[Value Investing]].

## Related Models
[[Probability]] · [[Opportunity Cost]] · [[Bayesian Updating]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Situations with extreme tail risks where utility is not linear.
- One-shot decisions with irreversible consequences requiring extra caution.
