# Mental Model: Pareto Principle

## Definition
The Pareto Principle, or 80/20 rule, observes that roughly 80% of outputs stem from 20% of inputs in many systems.

## Core Principle
Value is unevenly distributed; focusing on the vital few inputs yields outsized impact compared to spreading effort evenly.

## When To Use
- Prioritizing features, customers, or defect fixes.
- Time management, focusing on highest-leverage activities.

## Examples
- A handful of enterprise clients generating most revenue.
- A small number of bugs causing the majority of incidents.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Systems purposely balanced (e.g., egalitarian resource allocation).
- Regulatory contexts requiring equal treatment regardless of output.

## Sources
- Vilfredo Pareto’s wealth distribution studies; quality control (Juran).
