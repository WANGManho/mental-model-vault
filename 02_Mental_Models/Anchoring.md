# Mental Model: Anchoring

## Definition
Anchoring is the bias where the first piece of information sets a reference point that skews subsequent judgments.

## Core Principle
Early numbers, narratives, or framing exert outsize influence even if arbitrary, so later estimates gravitate toward the anchor.

## When To Use
- Negotiations, pricing, and salary discussions.
- Forecasting exercises where early guesses can bias teams.

## Examples
- A high initial offer sets expectations for contract value.
- Early project estimates anchoring planning even after scope changes.

## Related Models
[[Price Elasticity]] · [[Loss Aversion]] · [[Mental Accounting]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Blind auctions or sealed bids where anchors are hidden.
- Decisions supported by strong base rate data overriding early guesses.

## Sources
