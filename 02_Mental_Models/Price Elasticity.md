# Mental Model: Price Elasticity

## Definition
Price elasticity measures how sensitive demand or supply is to price changes.

## Core Principle
Elastic products see large quantity shifts with small price moves, while inelastic goods maintain demand despite cost changes.

## When To Use
- Pricing strategy, promotions, and subscription changes.
- Forecasting revenue impact from taxes or subsidies.

## Examples
- Airline tickets drop in price to stimulate off-season travel.
- Essential insulin shows inelastic demand even as prices rise.

## Related Models
[[Supply and Demand]] · [[Opportunity Cost]] · [[Tragedy of the Commons]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Monopoly or regulated pricing that ignores consumer preferences.
- Markets with hidden prices or bundling where elasticity is hard to observe.
