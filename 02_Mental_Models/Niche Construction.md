# Mental Model: Niche Construction

## Definition
Niche construction is the process by which organisms actively modify their environment, creating new niches that alter selection pressures.

## Core Principle
Agents can shape the landscape they operate in—designing rules, infrastructure, or habits—to favor their strengths.

## When To Use
- Platform strategies defining standards and APIs.
- Community building where norms and rituals change participation incentives.

## Examples
- Beavers building dams that create unique wetlands supporting their survival.
- Apple shaping the App Store rules to reinforce the [[Apple Ecosystem]].

## Related Models
[[Network Effects]] · [[Systems Thinking]] · [[Path Dependence]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Environments too rigid or regulated to be reshaped.
- Commoditized markets where differentiation is impossible.

## Sources
- Evolutionary biology and ecology research.
