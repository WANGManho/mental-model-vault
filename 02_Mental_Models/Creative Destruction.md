# Mental Model: Creative Destruction

## Definition
Creative destruction describes how innovation continually dismantles old industries while creating new ones.

## Core Principle
Entrepreneurial disruption reallocates capital and talent from incumbents to more productive uses, driving growth but inflicting localized pain.

## When To Use
- Assessing technology shifts that threaten moats.
- Timing investments in emerging versus legacy players.

## Examples
- Streaming services replacing physical media stores.
- Electric vehicles challenging combustion supply chains.

## Related Models
[[Value Investing]] · [[Path Dependence]] · [[Comparative Advantage]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Highly regulated sectors where change is throttled.
- Situations with insurmountable [[Network Effects]] anchoring incumbents.

## Sources
- Joseph Schumpeter's innovation theory.
