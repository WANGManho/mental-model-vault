# Mental Model: Stock and Flow

## Definition
Stock-and-flow diagrams distinguish between accumulations (stocks) and the rates that increase or decrease them (flows).

## Core Principle
Understanding how inflows, outflows, and delays change accumulation clarifies why interventions can have lagged or counterintuitive effects.

## When To Use
- Modeling user retention, cash balances, or inventory levels.
- Analyzing sustainability metrics such as carbon emissions.

## Examples
- Subscriber base as a stock fed by sign-ups and drained by churn.
- Cash runway determined by revenue inflows and expense outflows.

- Scenarios with instantaneous adjustments lacking meaningful stocks.
