# Mental Model: Network Effects

## Definition
Network effects occur when the value of a product or service increases as more users join, creating reinforcing adoption loops.

## Core Principle
Each additional node adds disproportionate utility to existing nodes, leading to compounding defensibility and potential winner-take-most dynamics.

## When To Use
- Evaluating platform businesses, marketplaces, or standards.
- Assessing virality or lock-in risk for partners.

## Examples
- Messaging platforms becoming more useful with every friend.
- The [[Amazon Flywheel]] tying customers, sellers, and logistics into a dense network.

## Related Models
[[Feedback Loops]] · [[Path Dependence]] · [[Compounding]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Negative network effects from overcrowding or spam.
- Regulatory fragmentation that caps network size.
