# Mental Model: Comparative Advantage

## Definition
Comparative advantage states that parties should specialize in activities where they have the lowest opportunity cost, even if another party is absolutely better at everything.

## Core Principle
Specialization and trade create surplus value because total output increases when each participant focuses on their relative strengths.

## When To Use
- Deciding which projects to own versus outsource.
- Negotiating partnerships or roles within a team.

## Examples
- Senior engineers focusing on architecture while juniors handle maintenance.
- Countries exporting goods where they are relatively more efficient.

## Related Models
[[Opportunity Cost]] · [[Incentives]] · [[Value Investing]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Over-specialization that limits adaptability.
- Industries with strong [[Network Effects]] where integration matters more than specialization.

## Sources
