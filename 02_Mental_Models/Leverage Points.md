# Mental Model: Leverage Points

## Definition
Leverage points are places within a complex system where a small shift can produce large changes.

## Core Principle
Not all interventions are equal; altering incentives, rules, or information flows often beats pushing harder on existing processes.

## When To Use
- Systems change, public policy, or organizational design.
- Prioritizing experiments when resources are limited.

## Examples
- Publishing transparent metrics to change behavior without coercion.
- Adjusting pricing tiers to steer customer mix instead of adding sales headcount.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Purely linear systems where every input scales proportionally.
- Blind tinkering without understanding of feedback structure.
