# Mental Model: Leverage

## Definition
Mechanical leverage uses a lever and fulcrum to amplify force, allowing a small input to move a larger load.

## Core Principle
Strategic placement of force, automation, or capital creates outsized output relative to effort when you identify long arms and optimal fulcrums.

## When To Use
- Prioritizing tools, IP, or platforms that scale work.
- Investing in processes that multiply the impact of a small team.

## Examples
- Software automation enabling one engineer to manage thousands of servers.
- Financial leverage magnifying returns (and risk) when borrowing at low rates.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Manual labor with no multipliers.
- Overleveraging that introduces fragility and blow-up risk.
