# Mental Model: Tragedy of the Commons

## Definition
The tragedy of the commons describes how shared resources are depleted when individuals act independently according to self-interest.

## Core Principle
Without governance or norms, each participant captures the benefits of exploitation while spreading costs across the group, leading to overuse.

## When To Use
- Managing shared infrastructure, APIs, or budgets.
- Designing incentives for environmental, social, or community assets.

## Examples
- Overgrazing common land until soil collapses.
- API rate limits abused by a single team, degrading system reliability.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Clear property rights or quotas aligning individual incentives with group outcomes.
- Cooperative cultures with strong monitoring and sanctions.
