# Mental Model: Time Delays

## Definition
Time delays are lags between taking an action and seeing its effects within a system.

## Core Principle
Delayed feedback can cause overshoot, oscillation, or complacency, so plans must anticipate lagged responses.

## When To Use
- Supply chains, hiring cycles, and engineering projects with long lead times.
- Policy changes or pricing moves where behavior shifts slowly.

## Examples
- Marketing campaigns that take weeks to generate pipeline.
- Infrastructure investments that pay off years after approval.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Real-time digital systems with immediate feedback.
- Highly automated loops where sensing and acting are instantaneous.
