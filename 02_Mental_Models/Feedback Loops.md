# Mental Model: Feedback Loops

## Definition
Feedback loops describe how outputs of a system are routed back as inputs, reinforcing (positive) or balancing (negative) future behavior.

## Core Principle
Understanding loops reveals how small actions propagate through systems and why interventions often have delayed or amplified effects.

## When To Use
- Diagnosing growth plateaus or runaway escalation.
- Designing flywheels, control systems, or policy interventions.

## Examples
- [[Amazon Flywheel]] accelerates selection, traffic, and lower prices.
- Product telemetry informing iterative releases.

## Related Models
[[Systems Thinking]] · [[Network Effects]] · [[Compounding]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Linear cause-effect assumptions that ignore loops.
- Short-term fixes that break balancing loops (e.g., subsidies creating shortages).
