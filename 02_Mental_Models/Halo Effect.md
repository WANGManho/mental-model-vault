# Mental Model: Halo Effect

## Definition
The halo effect occurs when a positive impression in one area influences our perception of unrelated traits.

## Core Principle
We extrapolate from limited positive cues (attractiveness, charisma, initial success) to broader judgments, ignoring contrary evidence.

## When To Use
- Hiring or performance reviews where first impressions dominate.
- Brand strategy and product line extensions.

## Examples
- Assuming a charismatic founder is also operationally excellent.
- Believing all products from a beloved brand share the same quality.

## Related Models
[[Confirmation Bias]] · [[Social Proof]] · [[Mental Accounting]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Structured evaluations with objective criteria and blind reviews.
- Data-driven decision processes that separate metrics from narrative.

## Sources
- Psych studies on impression formation.
