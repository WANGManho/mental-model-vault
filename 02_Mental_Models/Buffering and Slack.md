# Mental Model: Buffering and Slack

## Definition
Buffers and slack are extra capacity or inventory built into a system to absorb variability and prevent cascading failures.

## Core Principle
Deliberately underutilized resources increase resilience by decoupling interdependent steps and buying time to react.

## When To Use
- Capacity planning, incident response, and financial management.
- Designing guardrails for hiring, inventory, or compute resources.

## Examples
- Maintaining cash reserves to weather demand shocks.
- Using circuit breakers and queue depth limits to protect services.

## Related Models
[[Homeostasis]] · [[Cascading Failure]] · [[Margin of Safety]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Lean systems optimized for efficiency with zero slack.
- Environments where unused capacity is politically or economically unacceptable.

## Sources
- Operations management, resilience engineering, and biology.
