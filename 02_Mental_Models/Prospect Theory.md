# Mental Model: Prospect Theory

## Definition
Prospect theory models how people actually evaluate gains and losses, showing that losses loom larger than equivalent gains and that probabilities are distorted.

## Core Principle
Risk perception is shaped by reference points and framing; people are loss-averse and overweight small probabilities.

## When To Use
- Product pricing, insurance, and incentive design.
- Communicating risk or change initiatives to stakeholders.

## Examples
- Presenting savings as avoiding losses rather than achieving gains to motivate action.
- Lotteries succeeding because tiny probabilities are overweighted relative to expected value.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Highly trained quantitative decision-makers who adhere to rational utility theory.
- Machine-to-machine decisions unaffected by human psychology.

## Sources
