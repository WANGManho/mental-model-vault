# Mental Model: Sensitivity to Initial Conditions

## Definition
In chaotic systems, tiny differences in initial conditions can lead to drastically different outcomes over time.

## Core Principle
Precise forecasting becomes impossible beyond a horizon because small measurement errors grow exponentially, so focus shifts to ranges and resilience.

## When To Use
- Financial markets, weather, epidemiology, or innovation diffusion.
- Strategic planning where path dependency amplifies early moves.

## Examples
- Early customer feedback steering a product onto a distinct trajectory.
- Epidemiological models diverging based on minor contact-rate assumptions.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Linear systems with stable equilibria.
- Projects with highly deterministic processes and tight tolerances.
