# Mental Model: Market Signaling

## Definition
Market signaling explains how people communicate private information through costly actions or observable markers.

## Core Principle
Signals must be hard to fake—otherwise they convey no information—so actors invest in credentials, warranties, or product features to demonstrate quality.

## When To Use
- Interpreting certifications, guarantees, and pricing as information.
- Designing onboarding processes that screen for true commitment.

## Examples
- Luxury brands pricing high to signal exclusivity.
- Employers valuing degrees or portfolios to infer competence.

## Related Models
[[Principal-Agent Problem]] · [[Incentives]] · [[Value Investing]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Cheap talk where statements cost nothing and cannot be trusted.
- Environments with perfect information, removing the need for signals.
