# Mental Model: Supply and Demand

## Definition
Supply and demand describes how prices and quantities adjust until market supply equals market demand.

## Core Principle
When demand exceeds supply prices rise, attracting production; when supply exceeds demand prices fall, encouraging consumption until equilibrium emerges.

## When To Use
- Pricing new offerings or capacity constrained services.
- Evaluating how shocks ripple through commodity markets.

## Examples
- Chip shortages raising hardware prices until new fabs come online.
- Housing supply lagging urban demand, pushing rents up despite regulation.

## Related Models
[[Price Elasticity]] · [[Tragedy of the Commons]] · [[Opportunity Cost]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Heavy price controls that prevent equilibrium adjustments.
- Monopolies where a single producer disconnects price from underlying demand.
