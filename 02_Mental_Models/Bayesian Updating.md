# Mental Model: Bayesian Updating

## Definition
Bayesian updating revises the probability of a hypothesis as new evidence arrives, combining prior beliefs with likelihood of data.

## Core Principle
Beliefs should shift proportionally to how surprising the evidence is; strong priors move slowly, weak priors move quickly.

## When To Use
- Experimentation, diagnostics, and forecasting with sequential data.
- Personal learning loops that incorporate feedback.

## Examples
- A/B tests updating conversion expectations after each cohort.
- Medical diagnoses updating odds as lab results arrive.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Dogmatic environments that ignore evidence regardless of strength.
- Fully deterministic systems with no uncertainty.
