# Mental Model: Inverse Square Law

## Definition
The inverse square law states that certain physical effects (gravity, light, sound) decrease proportional to the square of the distance from the source.

## Core Principle
Influence drops off rapidly with distance, so doubling reach requires quadruple energy unless you change geometry or channels.

## When To Use
- Evaluating marketing reach, broadcast signals, or leadership span of control.
- Planning how attention or trust decays as organizations scale.

## Examples
- Headquarters influence on remote offices fading without deliberate touchpoints.
- Support for a message weakening as it travels through hierarchy layers.

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Digital networks with near-zero marginal distribution costs.
- Systems employing local leaders to reset distance decay.
