# Mental Model: Economies of Scale

## Definition
Economies of scale describe cost advantages gained when production volume increases, allowing fixed costs to be spread and processes to specialize.

## Core Principle
Large volumes unlock automation, bargaining leverage, and learning curves, reducing marginal cost and creating entry barriers.

## When To Use
- Evaluating manufacturing or logistics investments.
- Comparing incumbents with challengers in capital-intensive sectors.

## Examples
- [[Amazon Flywheel]] fulfillment centers lower per-package shipping costs as volume grows.
- Cloud providers amortizing data center spend across millions of customers.

## Related Models
[[Supply and Demand]] · [[Network Effects]] · [[Margin of Safety]]

## Opposite / Failure Cases
- Diseconomies of scale where coordination and bureaucracy overwhelm savings.
- Craft or luxury markets where scarcity is valued over volume.
